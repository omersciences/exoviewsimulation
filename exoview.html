How does night look like from Kepler-22b?

From Kepler-22b, a person would see a night sky filled with stars, though the constellations would differ from those on Earth due to the planet’s distant location in the galaxy. The Milky Way would still stretch across the sky, but with different star patterns. Depending on the planet’s atmosphere, the stars might appear brighter and more vivid, with no light pollution to obscure the view. If Kepler-22b had auroras, moons, or a ring system, these would add to the celestial display. However, nearby exoplanets like Kepler-186f or Kepler-62f might not be visible to the naked eye. The overall experience would offer a stunning, clear view of the cosmos.
